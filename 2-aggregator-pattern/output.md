## Story

At the edge of the bustling city, in the shadow of a lollipop-shaped tower, lived a rowdy group of Minions. Not just any Minions—these wore mismatched socks, carried umbrellas in the sunshine, and dreamed of becoming world-famous inventors.

Kevin, the self-proclaimed leader (whenever Bob wasn’t looking), stood on a soapbox made of marshmallows and addressed his crew. “Today, we invent something BIG! Something that will make even Gru proud!” His yellow companions cheered, tossing banana peels in the air.

Stuart, the musical one, strummed a tiny ukulele and hummed thoughtfully. “How about a gadget that makes everything taste like bananas?” The idea received raucous applause.

“Or a robot that does our chores?” suggested Dave, eyes wide with hope.

Bob, clutching his teddy bear, pointed to a squirrel twirling on a nearby bicycle. “Maybe… we make a flying machine!”

The Minions’ eyes sparkled with excitement. Blueprints were drawn—some with crayons, others with ketchup. Widgets and sprockets were scavenged from every nook and cranny of the Minion lair. Soon, the courtyard was filled with a marvelous mess: springs, balloons, feathers, and a suspiciously large blender.

Finally, the flying machine was ready—a hodgepodge contraption with teacup seats, propeller hats, and a banana-shaped rudder. The Minions climbed aboard, the crowd chanting “Banana! Banana!” as Kevin pulled a gigantic lever.

With a bang, a pop, and a wild loop-the-loop, the machine soared over the city, scattering glitter and giggles. People looked up and gasped as confetti rained and Minions waved, their yellow faces alight with joy.

It was a day no one would forget. For as the Minions learned, sometimes the greatest inventions are the ones that simply lift you—and everyone else—a little higher.

When they landed, squished together in a heap of feathers and laughter, Bob grinned. “Again?”

And so they did, because with Minions, the fun never really ends.

## Poem

In overalls of sapphire bright,
They scurry, giggling with all their might.
Yellow globes, a bouncing crew,
With eyes so wide, and spirits true.

A single eye, a gleeful stare,
They tumble forth without a care.
"Bello!" they cry, a joyous sound,
As mischief on this earth is found.

From banana peels to grand design,
Their loyalty, a curious sign.
A motley band, so small and bold,
A story in each antic told.
